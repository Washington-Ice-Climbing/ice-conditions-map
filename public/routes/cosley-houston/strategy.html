Most parties climbs this route car to car in 10-14 hours. The approach and descent are pretty straightforward, even if they have be done partially in the dark.

Most parties climb the first pitch with a little simul climbing to reach the crux. After pitching out the crux, parties will usually either solo or simul climb the rest of the route, except if they climb the second crux. Both the leader and follower should be solid at the grade because top belays can be marginal.

There are good camp options around 6000 ft at the base of the Coleman Glacier and slightly higher.

Flotation is usually helpful on the approach December through May. In early spring, the Grouse Creek approach will save time.
